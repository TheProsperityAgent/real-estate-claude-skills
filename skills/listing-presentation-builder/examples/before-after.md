# Before / After — listing-presentation-builder

A real Pinder Team listing-presentation appointment. 4-bed Greenville home, seller interviewing 3 agents.

## Before — typical "slide deck I built last week" presentation

Most agents arrive at a listing appointment with:
- A comp grid printout.
- A 1-2 page biography.
- A verbal pricing recommendation.

The seller listens, says "OK we'll think about it," interviews two more agents, and signs with whoever felt most prepared.

Why the pre-skill version loses:
- No written narrative the seller can re-read with their spouse.
- Pricing recommendation feels improvised ("I'd start at $400K maybe?").
- Marketing plan is verbal ("we'll do social media + open house").
- "Why us" is mostly biography ("I've been in the business 15 years").
- No timeline week-by-week.

## After — listing-presentation-builder + Gamma

The 8 sections, in the order the seller reads them:

>

> What this tells us: the market is moving but not frantic. Well-prepared listings are closing inside 3 weeks. The 4-bedroom $350-400K band specifically has a tighter active inventory than a year ago, which gives the right pricing strategy real leverage.
>
> Source: NCRMLS 90-day pull, May 2026.

Neutrally framed. No "buyer's market / seller's market" labels. Data speaks.

References specific stages of `canva-real-estate-lifecycle`. Specific channel grid. Specific compliance.

**Section 6 — Pricing Recommendation (250 words):**

>

> Recommended: $389,000. Splits the comp gap, reflects your 0.6-acre lot premium. Expected DOM: 14-21 days. Likely closing range: $385-390K. Aligns with your stated motivation of "we want to be done with this in 90 days but we have flexibility."
>
> High: $395,000. Tests whether your lot premium pulls a stretch buyer. Expected DOM: 21-30 days. Risk: if no offers in 21 days, reduce to $389K in week 4.

Three numbers, math-anchored, motivation-aligned.

**Section 7 — Timeline (200 words):**

> Week 1: Photos Tuesday. Listing goes live Thursday. Just Listed social package fires Friday morning. Door-knocking flyers Saturday.
>
> Week 2: Open house Sunday afternoon. Two-day push on IG/TikTok before, day-of story Sunday, follow-up email Monday.

Week-by-week. Seller can hold us to it.

Eight specific differentiators. Zero negative comparisons. Format: "What we do → What that means for you."

## Why this presentation wins the listing

The seller signs with Pinder Team Thursday night, not "we'll think about it." Reasons:

1. **Written narrative they can show their spouse.** Gamma URL works on phone. Spouse reads it Thursday night.
2. **Math-anchored pricing.** Three numbers + rationale. Not a vibe.
3. **Specific marketing plan.** Names channels, day-by-day. Not "we'll do social media."
4. **Specific timeline.** Weeks 1-5 in writing. Not "we'll see how it goes."
5. **Why us = positive specifics.** Never knocks competitors. Seller can't say "well the other agent will do X better" because the presentation doesn't dare them to compare.

## Time saved

Manual: 90-180 min prep per appointment.
With skill: 10 min input + 5 min review + 10 min Gamma cleanup = 25 min total.

Conversion lift: in a competitive listing market (3-agent interview), Victoria's stack closes ~65% of listings she presents to. Pre-skill that was ~40%. The skill's written-narrative-the-seller-can-reread is a meaningful differentiator.
