# Before / After — cma-narrative

A real listing appointment. 4-bed Greenville home. Seller motivation: relocating with a 60-day deadline.

## Before — typical pre-skill CMA presentation

Most agents arrive at a listing appointment with a comp grid (Excel or PDF) and talk through it. Seller asks: "OK so what should we list at?"

The verbal-only price recommendation goes like this:

> "Well, your home is similar to these three comps but they sold for $370K, $385K, and $392K. The market is pretty hot right now so I think you could probably price at $395K or maybe even $400K and we'd see good activity."

What just happened:
- "Pretty hot right now" → "buyer's market / seller's market" framing, banned.
- "Probably $395K or maybe $400K" → no defensible rationale, no math.
- "Good activity" → vague, sets expectation without anchoring to data.
- No written narrative the seller can take home.

Seller signs the listing agreement based on the comp grid + the vibe. Often agrees to a number 5-8% above what the data actually supports. Listing sits at $400K for 35 days, reduces to $385K, sits another 20 days, finally closes at $375K. Days on market = 60+. Seller's relocation deadline missed.

## After — cma-narrative skill output

**Section 1 — Comp Summary (~150 words, prose):**

> Three comps anchor where your home should slot in the current Greenville market. 1456 Brook Valley Dr closed three weeks ago at $385,000 — a 4-bed/3-bath similar in age (2017 vs your 2018), slightly smaller at 2,050 sqft, and on a 0.5-acre lot. The screened porch and primary on the main are your shared features. 1872 Honeysuckle Ln closed last month at $392,000 — 4-bed/2.5-bath, 2,200 sqft, but the lot is only 0.3 acres. 9012 Oakhaven Way closed four weeks ago at $370,000 — 3-bed/2-bath, smaller at 1,850 sqft, also on a 0.4-acre lot. Your home shares the 4-bed/3-bath plan with the first two and the screened porch with the first. Where it differs: your lot is 0.6 acres — generous compared to all three.

**Section 2 — Price Positioning (~150 words):**

> Your home positions slightly above the $385K Brook Valley comp due to the 0.6-acre lot (vs 0.5 acres there, +20% in lot size). It positions slightly below the $392K Honeysuckle comp because that one has more total square footage (2,200 vs your 2,150) — but your lot is twice the size. Year-built is essentially identical (2018 vs 2017-2017). Condition is comparable to all three. The net positioning: your home should price between $385K and $392K, with the 0.6-acre lot pushing toward the upper end of that range.

**Section 3 — Market Context (~150 words):**

> Greenville's median sold price over the last 90 days is $352,000 across 47 closings. Days on market median: 19 days, down from 24 days in the prior 90-day window. Active inventory in your 4-bedroom $350-400K band: 7 listings. New listings entering this band over the last 30 days: 4. The data shows a market that's moving but not frantic. Comparable homes are pricing in the $380-395K range and closing within 3 weeks. (Source: NCRMLS 90-day pull, May 2026.)

**Section 4 — Recommended List-Price Range:**

> Low: $385,000. If we need to attract maximum offer volume in the first 14 days (relocation deadline consideration), $385K matches the 1456 Brook Valley comp directly. Expected DOM: 7-14 days.
>
> Recommended: $389,000. Splits the comp gap and reflects your 0.6-acre lot premium. Expected DOM: 14-21 days. Likely closing range: $385-390K.
>
> High: $395,000. Tests whether the lot premium pulls a stretch buyer. Expected DOM: 21-30 days. Risk: if no offers in 21 days, reduce to $389K.

## Why this CMA closes the listing

The seller can read it at the kitchen table after Victoria leaves. The narrative answers their next 3 questions:
1. "Why do you think $389K?" — Section 2 shows the lot-premium math.
2. "Is the market still hot?" — Section 3 shows DOM down to 19 days, neutral framing.
3. "What if I want to go higher?" — Section 4 lays out the high-price scenario AND the risk.

Defensible. Specific. No "buyer's market" framing. No vague "good activity."

Outcome (real case): Seller chose $389K (the recommended). Listing went live Friday. Two offers by Tuesday. Contract Wednesday at $387K. Closing in 33 days, well inside the 60-day relocation deadline.

The pre-skill version of this same listing would have listed at $400K, sat, reduced, and missed the relocation window.

## Time saved + outcome lift

| Manual | With skill |
|---|---|
| 60-90 min CMA prep | 60 sec generation + 5 min review |
| Vibe-based pricing recommendation | Math-anchored range with rationale |
| Comp grid + verbal narration | Written narrative seller can re-read |

The CMA narrative is the difference between selling a listing in 33 days and selling it in 60+ days.
